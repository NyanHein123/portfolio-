Organizational Activity 1

At SP, the first solar photovoltaic recycling line is now being tested. The technique uses a machine to disassemble solar panels so that recyclable parts, such as glass and silver, can be found within. Its daily goal recycling capacity is 80 panels, with a maximum 95% recovery rate.

Organizational Activity 2

In order to create more resilient road surfaces, Samwoh, a Singaporean construction company, worked with Singapore Polytechnic. Initial studies demonstrate that this novel asphalt can exceed official chemical leaching standards while lasting at least 20% longer than conventional asphalt. With intentions to shortly conduct a road test, this is the first time plastic garbage has been used in Singapore in this way.

Explanation

Singapore's goals is to achieve net zero emission aspiration by 2025 and to follow the Singapore Green Plan 2030. The main purpose is to build a substainable furture. Acoording to the above data of energy balance, although it seems that the energy production and consumption is nearly equal, there is still carbon emissions. Moreover, from the waste statistics, it can be seen that there are many wastes which could not recycled. To achieve the goal mentioned above, all the organizations and residences in Singapore including Singapore Polytechnic and its students have to try to find ways to reduce energy consumption, to produce substainable and renewable energy, to reduce waste production and to recyle wastes.
