"Frequently Asked Questions About the environmental Issues"

Yes! Climate scientists agree: Humans significantly contribute to climate change by releasing excess greenhouse gases through activities like burning fossil fuels, farming, livestock, and deforestation. The Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change's Sixth Assessment Report confirms that human influence has unequivocally warmed the atmosphere, oceans, and land, leading to rapid and widespread changes across Earth's systems.

The effects of even a slight increase in global temperature—say, one or two degrees—are profound. Significant changes in local and regional climates can have an adverse effect on infrastructure, ecosystems, agriculture, public health, and water resources. There are more heat waves, more days over 90°F, and extreme weather events like storms, droughts, and floods, according to the 2018 National Climate Assessment. In addition, vulnerable places may be submerged if sea levels rise by one to four feet by the end of the century. Climate change must be addressed immediately in order to mitigate these effects.

By reducing their greenhouse gas emissions and getting ready for future changes, individuals can reduce their risk of climate change. The planet will be affected by the actions made today for many generations to come. Planning and development should incorporate climate change concerns while identifying and mitigating vulnerabilities. These steps protect vulnerable groups from the health and safety risks that climate change poses, including small children, the elderly, and the impoverished.

Delaying action on climate change escalates its detrimental effects on the planet and human health. Failure to act promptly may necessitate more extreme and costly measures to control greenhouse gases later. The latest National Climate Assessment reveals that global efforts to cut emissions can save tens of thousands of lives annually in the US by the century's end and prevent billions of dollars in damages related to water shortages, wildfires, agricultural losses, flooding, and other impacts. Taking immediate action can reduce climate risks while enhancing the environment, community infrastructure, transportation systems, and overall health.
